History of art: a brief
  • Ancient Art
  • Medieval Art
  • Renaissance
  • Baroque
  • Modern Art
Ancient Art

Religious symbolism, storytelling, and depictions of everyday life.

Use of natural materials like pigments, animal bones, and cave walls.

Lascaux cave paintings, Egyptian pyramids, Mesopotamian sculptures.
  • Rise and fall of civilizations, development of agriculture and religion, technological advancements like metalworking and writing.
  • Served as a means of storytelling, recording rituals and beliefs, and communicating with the supernatural.
Medieval Art

Religious themes, primarily Christianity, often serving didactic purposes.

Flattened perspective, stylized figures, and vibrant colors.

Byzantine mosaics, Romanesque architecture, Gothic stained glass windows.
  • Spread of Christianity, rise of the Catholic Church, feudalism, Crusades, Black Death.
  • Primarily religious in nature, used to educate and inspire faith, glorify rulers and the church, and depict biblical stories.
Renaissance

Revival of classical ideals, emphasis on humanism, naturalism, and perspective.

Realistic portrayal of figures, use of light and shadow, and exploration of anatomy.

Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa, Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel ceiling, Raphael's The School of Athens.
  • Rediscovery of classical texts and ideals, rise of humanism and individualism, invention of the printing press, exploration and colonialism.
  • Celebrated human potential, naturalism, and beauty, reflected changing values and the growing importance of secular themes.
Baroque

Drama, movement, and religious fervor.

Use of chiaroscuro (light and shadow), grand scale, and emotional intensity.

Caravaggio's The Calling of Saint Matthew, Rembrandt's Night Watch, Rubens' The Rape of the Daughters of Leucippus.
  • Reformation and Counter-Reformation, religious conflicts, absolutism, scientific revolution, exploration and colonization.
  • Employed drama, emotion, and grandeur to evoke religious devotion, reinforce the power of the church and state, and glorify rulers and patrons.
Modern Art

Experimentation, breaking away from traditional forms and embracing subjective expression.

Diverse styles and movements, including Impressionism, Post-Impressionism, Cubism, Surrealism, Abstract Expressionism, Pop Art, and more.
